
# Agile & Scrum Overview

## 1. Agile Methodology

Agile is an **iterative and incremental approach** to software development.

### Key Characteristics:
- Focus on **customer satisfaction**
- Deliver a **working piece of functionality in every iteration**
- **Requirements can change at any stage** of development
- Agile easily accommodates **mid-development requirement changes**
- Less emphasis on **heavy design and documentation**
- More focus on **fast and continuous delivery of working software**

---

## 2. Agile Frameworks

Agile is implemented using various frameworks:

- **Kanban**
- **Scrum**
- **Extreme Programming (XP)**
- **Crystal**

These frameworks help teams apply Agile principles effectively.

### Common Agile Practices:

#### Daily Scrum (Stand-up Meeting)
- Short meeting (usually **15 minutes**)
- Team members discuss:
  - What they did yesterday
  - What they will do today
  - Any blockers

#### Sprint Review
- Conducted at the end of a sprint
- Team gives a **demo to the Product Owner**
- Showcases completed work

#### Sprint Retrospective
- Discussion on:
  - ✅ What went well
  - ❌ What went wrong
  - 🔧 Areas for improvement

#### Burndown Chart
- Tracks:
  - **Work completed**
  - **Work remaining**
- Helps monitor sprint progress

---

## 3. Scrum Framework

Scrum is a **framework used to build software products following Agile principles**.

### Scrum Team Roles:

- **Product Owner**
  - Defines requirements
  - Prioritizes the product backlog
  - Represents the customer

- **Scrum Master**
  - Facilitates Scrum process
  - Removes obstacles
  - Ensures the team follows Agile practices

- **Development Team**
  - Builds the product
  - Includes developers and engineers

- **QA Team (Quality Assurance)**
  - Tests the software
  - Ensures quality and stability

---

## Summary

Agile focuses on **flexibility, collaboration, and continuous delivery**.  
Scrum is one of the most popular frameworks used to implement Agile effectively through structured roles, events, and practices.
